Khushi Baby Association

About the Organization:
Khushi Baby is a digital‚Äêhealth non‚Äêprofit headquartered in Udaipur, Rajasthan. Their Community Health Integrated Platform (CHIP) centers on the ‚ÄúKhushi Necklace,‚Äù a wearable RFID tag that stores immunization and maternal health records for mothers and infants. The data syncs via mobile applications to give frontline health workers up-to-date patient information, improving immunization rates and reducing missed appointments. Since inception, Khushi Baby has empowered over 75,000 health workers and reached more than 48 million beneficiaries across rural India.

Project: ASHA Saheli
ASHA Saheli is a last-mile healthcare delivery platform co-developed with A4I. Building on Khushi Baby‚Äôs wearable technology, ASHA Saheli equips Accredited Social Health Activists (ASHAs) with a mobile app to track maternal and child health metrics, schedule vaccinations, and identify high-risk cases. Leveraging AI-driven alerts and GIS mapping, ASHA Saheli ensures timely interventions for pregnant women and newborns in underserved villages. The platform integrates with state health databases to maintain continuity of care and facilitate data-driven decision making at scale.

Sikshana Foundation

About the Organization:
Established in 2002 and based in Bangalore, Sikshana Foundation works to improve learning outcomes in government primary schools across Karnataka. They partner with state education departments, local communities, and volunteers to deliver teacher training, foundational literacy and numeracy programs, and ICT integration. Their data-driven approach has consistently led to measurable gains in student performance, with many districts adopting Sikshana‚Äôs model as their standard best practice.

Project: Shiksha Copilot
Shiksha Copilot is an AI-powered teaching assistant that provides real-time lesson planning, automated remedial content, and individualized feedback to teachers. Integrated into the existing classroom ecosystem, it uses natural language processing to analyze student responses and suggest differentiated activities. This tool empowers educators to address learning gaps proactively and foster a supportive learning environment in resource- constrained settings.

Vision Empower Trust

About the Organization:
Vision Empower Trust is a Karnataka-based non-profit incubated at IIIT Bangalore. Their mission is to empower children with visual impairment through inclusive STEM education. By designing accessible Braille and audio resources, they train teachers in tactile learning methods and develop assistive technologies‚Äîsuch as tactile diagrams and audio-based STEM kits‚Äîso that visually impaired students can fully engage with science and mathematics curricula. Vision Empower‚Äôs innovative programs have been recognized by national education bodies and deployed in over 50 schools across South India.

Project: Shiksha SEEDS
Good quality access to early education and introduction to digital technologies empowers visually impaired children and prepares them for better opportunities for learning, education, and good careers. Many of these children go to special schools for Children with Disabilities (CwDs) where many of the teachers are also people with disabilities with limited technology awareness and access. Operating in 15 Indian states, Vision Empower Trust supports over 3,000 visually impaired students and 1,555 teachers across 134 schools. The Shiksha SEEDS initiative will leverage this existing infrastructure to enhance educational access and learning outcomes for visually impaired learners, by making key Gen AI enhancements to Subodha (existing accessible LMS at VE), such as voice-enabled content search in various local languages, building video content repository and accessible UX, and overall ensuring inclusive participation for these teachers and children in the digital education ecosystem.
